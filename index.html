PROJECT 01 / GRADUATION PROJECT
🏆 1st Place — SCU Innovation Award 2026

Unmanned Surface Vessel — Offshore Oil Spill Recovery

Lead Mechanical Design & Systems Integration Engineer

End-to-end design, manufacture, and commissioning of an autonomous catamaran-hull USV for oil spill recovery in Port Said coastal waters. The vessel integrates an open-belt oil skimmer, cyclonic oil/water separator, onboard RPi4B navigation system, AI visual recognition camera, and dual high-efficiency thrusters. LOA 1.5 m · Beam 1.0 m · Design speed 10 kn · 107 kg full-load capacity.

Hull form developed in MAXSURF; hydrostatic curves, GM, and stability envelope verified across full load range using MAXSURF Stability — 20 cm draft enables access to shallow sensitive zones.
SolidWorks FEA on cross-deck and hull-frame joints under combined hydrostatic + wave-slam loading; stress concentrations resolved via gusset reinforcement.
CFD wave-resistance analysis of catamaran demihulls; pressure contour plots used to optimise hull spacing and bow form for reduced interference drag.
GRP demihulls laminated over CNC-cut foam/plywood male moulds; propulsion shaft tunnels jig-drilled to ±0.5° angle tolerance.
Led multidisciplinary team through full-scale system integration, float tests, and operational sea trials with oil skimmer and cyclonic separator running.
MAXSURF SolidWorks FEA CFD Wave Resistance GRP Lamination CNC Hydrostatics RPi4B Navigation Systems Integration

PROJECT 02 / CFD ANALYSIS

Formula Student Car — Aerodynamic CFD Analysis

CFD Engineer — SCU Racing Team

External aerodynamic analysis of the SCU Formula Student car using SolidWorks Flow Simulation. Pressure and velocity contour plots were generated across the full vehicle body including front wing, bodywork, and rear wing surfaces to evaluate downforce and drag performance.

Cut-plane pressure contour plots (3 planes) and flow trajectory visualisations generated at 215 solver iterations to confirm convergence.
Pressure range 100,817–102,183 Pa mapped across the car body, identifying high-pressure stagnation zones at the nose and low-pressure suction regions under wings.
Results used to inform wing angle and bodywork geometry decisions for competition setup, targeting maximum downforce-to-drag ratio.
SolidWorks Flow Sim External Aerodynamics Pressure Contours Flow Trajectories Downforce Analysis

Racing Vehicle Steering Systems

Head of Steering Committee — SCU Racing Team

PROJECT 04

Cold Rolling Machine Prototype

End-to-End Project Lead

Full-cycle design, manufacture, and commissioning of a bench-top metal cold rolling machine for academic research. The machine features a rigid welded C-frame, adjustable roll-gap mechanism, and chain/gear drive train, achieving verified thickness reduction on mild steel strip samples.

Welded C-frame FEA under maximum roll-separation force; bearing housing deflection constrained to sub-millimetre to preserve roll parallelism across the full strip width.
Roll barrels turned to h6 tolerance; journals ground to interference fit for bearing housings. Full-face alignment verified using dial gauge before commissioning.
Drive shaft sized via combined bending-torsion analysis using ASME shaft design method; key/keyway joints designed to BS 4235 standard fits.

PROJECT 05

Competition-Grade ROV — Hull Sealing

ROV Mechanical Committee — Sealing Lead

Engineered the complete pressure-sealing architecture for a competition ROV, covering static O-ring face seals, cable gland penetrations, and dynamic lip seals at all thruster shaft entries — designed to ISO 3601 standards and verified by hydrostatic pressure testing.

O-ring groove dimensions calculated to ISO 3601 for 15–25% compression ratio; dynamic gland profiles selected for rotary shaft conditions.
Hydrostatic pressure test conducted at 1.25× maximum operational depth equivalent — zero leakage across all sealing interfaces after assembly.
Ceramic shaft sleeves specified at dynamic rotary penetrations; surface finish Ra < 0.4 µm maintained to lip-seal compatibility requirements.
Zero hull integrity failures across all competition runs. System delivered to competition-ready status ahead of submission deadline.
